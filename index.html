MySystem Pre-prototype Demo

To try the demo out, drag the objects at left into the main field, then connect them with arrows by dragging from one terminal to another. Try using this demo space to create a MySystem diagram for the energy story below:


"Jimmy made some breakfast on a lazy Saturday morning. He turned on the stove, put some water in a pot and heated it until it boiled. He took an egg out of the refrigerator and put it in the boiling water. When he was done, he enjoyed a perfect hard-boiled egg."


Make a diagram showing how energy was transformed from one form to another and transferred from one place to another while the egg was in the boiling water. Add a label to each energy transformation describing the process. Also label each arrow with the form of energy being transferred.


Note: Where energy is lost to the surroundings, indicate this loss by showing energy being transferred to the world image.

Welcome to the MySystem pre-prototype demo

What this example does :

  • * Provides a field for composing a MySystem diagram
  • * Provides sample images of objects where energy is transformed
  • * Enables objects to be connected by arrows to show how energy is transferred
  • * Provides comment boxes

How to use it :

  • * Drag and drop objects from the left-hand sidebar to the main field.
  • * Connect the modules with arrows by dragging from one button to another.

Planned features for the beta and final versions:

  • * Diagrams will be able to be saved, shared and embedded into blogs or Web pages
  • * Embedded assessment is integrated into the diagramming process
  • * A library of images will be available, with the ability to upload images
  • * Blocks and connecting arrows will be label-able
  • * Arrow widths will be able to be varied to indicate quantities
  • * Colors or textures of arrows will be changeable to indicate the type of energy transferred
  • * Objects will be able to contain subsystems within them, and students will zoom in to view and edit these subsystems
  • * Quantities of energy and efficiencies of transformation will be displayable
  • * Units will be able to be tracked and converted
